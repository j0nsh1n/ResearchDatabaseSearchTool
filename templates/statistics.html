Duplicate Detection

 
Finds near-identical papers across your sources so you can keep one copy of each.
 Removed copies are screened out, never deleted.

 

  How detection and auto-resolve work
  
Detection needs prepared papers (Data Management, step 4). Match strictness:
  higher values find only near-identical articles, lower values find more loosely related ones.
  Auto-Resolve All keeps the most complete abstract in each group; if lengths are
  similar it prefers higher-quality sources (PubMed > Europe PMC > CrossRef > OpenAlex > …).
  Redundant copies are screened out (hidden from Search, not deleted) and the screening report counts
  update. You can restore anything from its cluster on the Clusters page, or use per-group
  Keep this to choose the winner by hand.
