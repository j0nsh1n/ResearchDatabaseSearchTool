Data Management

Step 1 of 4. Pick topics, choose sources, and fetch papers - the app prepares them for search automatically.

1. Select Topics

Choose the academic areas you care about - we use them to recommend sources.

How topics are used

Topics drive two things: which databases get recommended below, and the coverage map after a fetch (are you missing sources teachers often expect for this topic?). Your collection stays private to this account.

2. Choose Sources

Select topics above to see recommended sources, or choose manually below.

About the sources

Each source is a free academic database. Some need free API keys (CORE, NASA ADS); without a key they simply skip - the rest still run.

3. Fetch Articles

Searches every selected source at once and saves the papers to your private collection. When the fetch finishes, papers are prepared for search automatically.

How fetching works

The query runs against every selected source in parallel as a background job (progress bar; if one fetch is already running you will be told to wait). Saves title, abstract, authors, year, and journal. Records without an abstract are skipped (later steps need text). After a successful fetch the app starts the prepare step (embeddings) for you; you can watch its progress in the card below. Your last query, sources, and email are remembered in this browser.

Advanced fetch options

Coverage map

A quick health check after a fetch: papers per source, and which recommended sources are still empty.

4. Prepare Papers for Search

This step runs automatically after each fetch, and the analysis model is picked to match your selected topics. Use this card to re-run it or switch models.

What preparing does (embeddings)

Preparing creates embeddings: each abstract becomes a numerical "meaning fingerprint" that powers Search (hybrid ranking, seed, and more-like-starred), Clusters, and Duplicate detection. It runs as a background job with a progress bar (you can leave the page open) and uses your GPU when available. The model is chosen automatically from your topics (biomedical topics get a biomedical model, hard-science topics a scientific-paper model, anything else a fast general one) - pick a different one under Advanced to override. Only prepare new papers turns on automatically when you choose Add to collection (existing fingerprints are kept); for a full Replace fetch it stays off so everything is processed. Switching models reprocesses everything.
