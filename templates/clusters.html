Article Clusters

Group your articles into themed clusters using their embeddings, then browse each cluster.

Generate Clusters

Clustering groups your articles by topic similarity using their embeddings — create embeddings on the Data Management page first. Leave Auto on and the app tries a range of cluster counts and keeps the one that separates topics most cleanly. Turn Auto off to set the number yourself: too few mixes topics together, too many splits them apart. K-Means is fast and works well in most cases; Hierarchical (Ward) can give cleaner groups on smaller collections. Re-clustering replaces the groups but keeps your exclusions.

Each cluster's label is built from the terms that most distinguish it from the other clusters, so no keyword appears in two labels. Use the clusters to triage your collection: Exclude cluster screens out an entire off-topic group at once, and each article inside has its own Exclude/Restore toggle. Screened-out articles are hidden from Search results and exports (and shown crossed out here) — nothing is deleted, and Restore brings them back anytime.
