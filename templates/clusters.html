How clustering works and choosing a method

Clustering uses the embeddings from Data Management, so prepare papers first (that job can finish in the background). Density (default) finds natural topics itself and parks odd papers in an "outliers" group - you do not choose a count. Prefer a fixed number of groups? Switch to K-Means or Hierarchical, then use Auto (app picks the cleanest count) or uncheck Auto and set the slider yourself. Re-clustering replaces groups but keeps exclusions (and your screening report still reflects them).

More about labels, overviews, and screening

Each cluster's label uses terms that most distinguish it from other clusters (no shared keyword spam). Opening a cluster shows a short topic overview (year span + example titles) so you can decide if the group is on-topic. Excluded papers are screened out of Search and hybrid ranking entirely; counts by exclusion reason appear on the Duplicates page's screening report for hand-ins.
